Formula Explanations

Our tool uses standard accounting formulas to ensure accuracy for small and medium-sized businesses:

Gross Margin % = ((Selling Price - Cost Price) / Selling Price) × 100
Markup % = ((Selling Price - Cost Price) / Cost Price) × 100
Profit Amount = Selling Price - Cost Price

Margin vs. Markup: What's the Difference?

Many business owners confuse these two concepts. Markup is the percentage added to the cost to reach a selling price. Margin is the percentage of the final selling price that is profit. Margin is always lower than markup for the same transaction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a 50% margin good?

In many retail industries, a 50% margin is standard (keystone pricing). However, your "good" margin depends on your industry's operating expenses and competition.

Does this include taxes?

This calculator focuses on gross margin (Price - Cost). For net profit, you would need to subtract additional expenses like taxes, shipping, and marketing.
