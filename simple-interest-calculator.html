Simple vs. Compound Interest

The primary difference between these two lies in the "compounding" effect. While simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal, compound interest is calculated on the principal *plus* any interest that has accumulated in previous periods. Over long durations, compound interest grows significantly faster than simple interest.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a simple interest calculator?

It is a digital tool that calculates the interest charge or earnings on a principal sum over a specific time period using a fixed interest rate.

When should I use simple interest?

Simple interest is typically used for short-term loans, car loans, and certain types of fixed-rate certificates of deposit where interest is paid out rather than reinvested.

Can I calculate interest for months?

Yes. Our calculator allows you to toggle between years and months. If months are selected, the tool automatically divides the duration by 12 to convert it to the required annual time unit for the formula.
