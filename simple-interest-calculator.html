How This Calculator Works

Simple interest is calculated by multiplying the principal amount by the annual interest rate and then by the time period in years. Unlike compound interest, simple interest is only charged on the original principal.

Formula: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time / 100

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Principal?

The principal is the initial amount of money borrowed or invested, before any interest is added.

When is Simple Interest used?

It is commonly used for short-term loans, car loans, and some types of consumer credit.

How is it different from Compound Interest?

Compound interest is calculated on both the principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods.
