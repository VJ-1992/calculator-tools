Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main difference between Simple and Compound interest?

Simple interest is calculated only on the initial amount (principal) you invested or borrowed. Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus any interest that has accumulated from previous periods.

When is Simple Interest typically used?

It is most common in short-term loans, car loans, and some types of certificates of deposit (CDs) where interest is paid out rather than reinvested.

Is this calculator accurate for bank loans?

Most modern bank mortgages and savings accounts use compounding interest. This tool is perfect for private loans or specific fixed-rate financial products that explicitly state "simple interest."

How do I calculate for only a few days?

Enter the number of days as a decimal of a year. For example, for 30 days, you could enter approx 0.082 years (30 / 365).
